Biryani

Biryani is a celebrated, aromatic rice dish originating from the Indian subcontinent, renowned for its complex layering of fragrant long-grain basmati rice, marinated meat (typically chicken, mutton, or goat), and a rich blend of spices like saffron, cardamom, and cinnamon. The dish is traditionally prepared using the dum pukht method, where ingredients are slow-cooked in a sealed vessel to trap steam and infuse deep flavors. While its name is derived from the Persian word birian (meaning "fried before cooking"), biryani evolved into a diverse culinary icon with distinct regional varieties, such as the spicy Hyderabadi, the subtle and fragrant Lucknowi, and the Kolkata version, which uniquely includes potatoes and boiled eggs.

Ingredients

  • Basmati rice
  • Vegetables or meat
  • Onion
  • Tomato
  • Yogurt
  • Ginger-garlic paste
  • Biryani spices
  • Oil or ghee
  • Salt
  • Water

Steps

  1. Wash and soak basmati rice.
  2. Cook vegetables or meat with spices.
  3. Partially cook the rice.
  4. Layer rice and curry together.
  5. Cook on low heat until fully done.
