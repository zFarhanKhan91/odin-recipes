Dosa

Dosa is a staple South Indian savory crepe made from a fermented batter of ground rice and black gram (urad dal), prized for its crispy edges and soft, slightly tangy interior. Originating over 1,500 years ago in the Tamil and Kannada regions, it has evolved into numerous popular varieties, including the potato-stuffed Masala Dosa, the lacy and quick Rava Dosa made with semolina, and the fluffy, stacked Set Dosa. Typically served hot with sambar and various chutneys, it is celebrated as a nutritious, gluten-free dish rich in probiotics and plant-based protein.

Ingredients

Dosa

  • Rice
  • Urad Dal
  • Water
  • Salt
  • Oil

Sambar

  • Toor dal (piegon peas)
  • Mixed vegetables
  • Tamarind
  • Sambar Powder
  • Salt
  • Water
  • Oil

Coconut Chutney

  • Grated Coconut
  • Green chili
  • Ginger
  • Salt
  • Water

Steps

Dosa

  1. Soak rice and urad dal in water.
  2. Grind into a smooth batter.
  3. Ferment the batter overnight.
  4. Add salt and mix well.
  5. Spread batter on a hot pan and cook until crispy.

Sambar

  1. Cook toor dal until soft.
  2. Boil vegetables in water.
  3. Add tamarind extract and spices.
  4. Mix cooked dal and simmer well.
  5. Serve Hot.

Coconut Chutney

  1. Grind Coconut, chili, and ginger.
  2. Add water to make a smooth plate.
  3. Add salt and mix well.
  4. Server fresh.
